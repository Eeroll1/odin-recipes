Description

A Nordic dish, also known as graved salmon. The name of the dish comes from the way it was originally made: the freshly caught fish was rubbed with salt, sugar and herbs and then buried for days to let it cure. Works great as a side dish or on a piece of rye bread. Especially popular at Christmas

Ingredients

  • 700g fillet of salmon
  • 1.5 tbsp of seasalt
  • 1-2 tsp. of sugar
  • Dill

    • Steps

    1. Place fillet on a chopping board skin down. Remove bones
    2. Season evenly with salt, sugar and dill
    3. Wrap the fish tightly in foil/baking paper and cling film
    4. Place in a fridge and let cure for at least a day. Turn a couple times to ensure even seasoning.
    5. Remove any excess seasoning from the surface of the fish
    6. Cut into thin strips
